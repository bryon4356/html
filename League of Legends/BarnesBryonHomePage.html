Network Administration

What is a network administrator? A network administrator is someone who is in control and upkeep of a network. This person has intricate knowledge of the network they are in control of but the underlying principles of how networks work. This includes how WANs, LANs, VLANs, and VPNs work. They have the ability to deploy and reconfigure multiple access points, routers and switches within their network.

Certifications and training

All Network administrators recieve the same base line training to be considered Network administrators. They start with Comptia IT fundamentals and work their way up the pyramid to Network +, Security + and finishing with CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate). These certifications show potential employers that they have a base knowledge and understanding of how any network runs and can diagnose a multitude of issues. To find more information about the certifications needed to be a network administrator click here.

Salary and Benifits

Working as a Network administrator the pay and benifits are usually very good. The average starting salary is around $40,000 and can increase based on schooling and certifications that you have. The experts in the field can earn over $130,000 a year. Not only is the pay very good but with the benifits of usually having stellar health and dental plans and the ability to work from home is the best. Being able to do your job from the comfort of your home makes this a very desierable job. You can see more about the levels of salary in the coming pages.
